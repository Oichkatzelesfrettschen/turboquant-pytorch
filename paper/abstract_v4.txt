Title: NSN-TurboQuant: Distribution-Aware KV Cache Compression
with 136bp Quality Gain and 69% Memory Reduction

LLM key-value (KV) cache memory grows linearly with context length
and dominates inference cost. We identify that the primary quality
bottleneck in KV cache quantization is distribution mismatch:
real caches have per-channel outliers with 423x variance ratio,
while Lloyd-Max codebooks assume Gaussian inputs.

We extend TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) with three techniques. First,
Normalize-Shift-Normalize (NSN) pre-processing reduces the variance
ratio to 1.01x (KL divergence 0.02 from Gaussian), enabling
codebooks to operate at their design optimum. Second, materialized
Walsh-Hadamard rotation achieves cuBLAS GEMM throughput (561M
vectors/second with TF32 on Ada) at 64x fewer parameters than
dense Haar rotation. Third, bit-packed QJL signs reduce residual
correction storage 8x.

Ablation on Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B shows the full pipeline
achieves 0.9985 attention cosine similarity (+136 basis points over
vanilla) with 69% memory reduction. NSN alone accounts for +136bp
of the gain; rotation choice contributes <1bp -- a surprising
finding that distribution normalization, not decorrelation method,
determines quantization quality.

We ground the rotation design in Cayley-Dickson algebra with 30
algebraic properties formally verified via Z3 SMT solver, and
identify shared Hadamard butterfly structure enabling potential
fused rotation-multiplication hardware.
