Title: Distribution-Aware KV Cache Quantization with Algebraically
Structured Rotations and Bit-Packed Residual Correction

Key-value (KV) cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference cost.
We show that the primary bottleneck for quantization quality is not the
rotation method or codebook choice, but the mismatch between the
channel-outlier distribution of real KV caches and the Gaussian
assumption underlying optimal scalar quantizers.

Our pipeline applies Normalize-Shift-Normalize (NSN) pre-processing to
transform KV cache distributions to near-N(0,1/d) (KL divergence 0.02),
enabling Lloyd-Max codebooks to operate at their design optimum. We
combine this with materialized Walsh-Hadamard rotation via cuBLAS GEMM
(matching dense random rotation throughput at 64x fewer parameters) and
bit-packed QJL sign storage (8x sign memory reduction).

Ablation on Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B reveals NSN as the dominant
contributor: +136 basis points cosine similarity and +25 percentage
points top-1 attention match over vanilla TurboQuant (ICLR 2026).
The full pipeline achieves 0.9985 attention cosine (vs 0.9849 vanilla)
with 69% KV cache memory reduction.

We ground the rotation design in Cayley-Dickson algebra, proving
isometry for quaternion and octonion block rotations (30/36 properties
Z3-verified). The shared Hadamard butterfly structure between WHT
rotation and Cariow's algebraic multiplication factorization
(Cariow-Hadamard duality) suggests fused hardware implementations.
