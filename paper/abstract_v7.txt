Title: Distribution-Aware KV Cache Compression via Algebraically
Structured Quantization Pipelines

KV cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference. We present a
distribution-aware quantization pipeline that achieves 0.9985 attention
cosine similarity (vs 0.9849 for TurboQuant) with 69% memory reduction
by addressing the root cause of quality loss: the 423x per-channel
variance ratio in real KV caches, which defeats codebooks designed
for Gaussian distributions.

Our key insight is that distribution normalization -- not rotation or
codebook design -- determines quantization quality. We demonstrate this
through a systematic decomposition: Normalize-Shift-Normalize (NSN)
contributes +136 basis points cosine similarity and +25pp top-1 match,
while rotation method (Haar, Walsh-Hadamard, Cayley-Dickson block)
contributes less than 1bp regardless of algebraic structure.

Based on this finding, we construct an optimized pipeline:
NSN pre-processing, materialized Walsh-Hadamard rotation for cuBLAS
throughput at 64x fewer parameters, and bit-packed QJL residual signs
(8x sign storage reduction). We explore the rotation design space
through Cayley-Dickson algebra, proving isometric guarantees for
structured block rotations and identifying shared Hadamard butterfly
structure between rotation and algebraic multiplication that enables
fused hardware implementations.
