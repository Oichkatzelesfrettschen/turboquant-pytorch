Title: Structured Rotations and Lattice Quantization for KV Cache
Compression via Cayley-Dickson Algebra

Abstract (250 words max for MICRO):

Key-value (KV) cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference cost.
TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) addresses this via random orthogonal rotation,
per-coordinate Lloyd-Max quantization, and 1-bit QJL residual correction.
We identify three limitations: O(d^2) Haar rotation cost, scalar
quantization ignoring vector structure, and no pre-processing for the
hostile channel-outlier distribution of real KV caches.

We introduce a pipeline combining (1) Normalize-Shift-Normalize (NSN)
pre-processing that transforms channel-outlier distributions into
near-N(0,1/d), enabling optimal Lloyd-Max codebook utilization;
(2) Walsh-Hadamard rotation materialized as a dense matrix for cuBLAS
GEMM at full GPU throughput with O(d) parameter storage; and (3)
bit-packed QJL sign storage for 69% memory reduction. We ground these
techniques in a unified algebraic framework connecting Cayley-Dickson
algebras, Clifford algebras, E8 lattice quantization, and the
Barnes-Wall lattice hierarchy.

Ablation on Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B KV caches shows NSN is the
dominant contributor (+136 basis points cosine similarity, +25
percentage points top-1 attention match), while structured rotation
provides formal isometry guarantees and storage efficiency. Our
full-stack pipeline achieves 0.9985 attention cosine similarity (vs
0.9849 vanilla) with 69% memory reduction. We formally verify 30 of
36 algebraic properties via Z3 SMT solver and identify the
Cariow-Hadamard duality between structured rotation and algebraic
multiplication, opening paths to fused hardware implementations.
