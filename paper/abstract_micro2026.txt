Title: Architecture-Aware KV Cache Quantization:
Distribution Normalization with Structured Rotation Dispatch

Abstract:

KV cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference, scaling
linearly with context length. We present an architecture-aware
quantization pipeline with three contributions.

First, we identify that per-channel variance ratios up to 423x in
real KV caches render Gaussian-optimal codebooks suboptimal.
Normalize-Shift-Normalize pre-processing reduces this ratio to
1.01x, improving attention cosine similarity by 136 basis points
and top-1 match by 25 percentage points on Qwen2.5-3B and
Mistral-7B -- the dominant quality factor in our ablation.

Second, we implement architecture-aware execution: materialized
Walsh-Hadamard rotation via cuBLAS with automatic TF32 dispatch
(3x throughput on Ampere+) at 64x fewer parameters than dense
Haar rotation, and bit-packed QJL signs reducing per-score
attention bandwidth from 256B to 34B. Five-tier GPU dispatch
(Hopper through generic) auto-selects precision modes.

Third, we ground the rotation design space in Cayley-Dickson
algebra, proving isometric guarantees for structured block
rotations (55 statements across 30 proof groups, SMT-verified).
Ablation reveals a
surprising dichotomy: distribution normalization determines
quality (>99% of gain) while architecture-aware dispatch
determines throughput.
