Title: Distribution Normalization Dominates Rotation Choice in
KV Cache Quantization: An Ablation Study

LLM key-value (KV) cache memory grows linearly with context length.
We conduct a systematic ablation of TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) KV cache
compression and find that distribution pre-processing, not the
rotation method, determines quantization quality.

Real KV caches exhibit per-channel outliers where the maximum
per-coordinate variance exceeds the median by 423x. We show that
Normalize-Shift-Normalize (NSN) pre-processing reduces this ratio to
1.01x, improving attention cosine similarity by 136 basis points and
top-1 match by 25 percentage points on Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B.
In contrast, replacing the dense Haar rotation with structured
Walsh-Hadamard adds less than 1bp -- though it reduces rotation
parameter storage 64x and enables cuBLAS-speed application via matrix
materialization.

We complement the ablation with bit-packed QJL sign storage (8x sign
memory reduction, 69% total KV memory reduction) and ground the
rotation design space in Cayley-Dickson algebra, proving isometric
guarantees for quaternion and octonion block rotations (30 properties
SMT-verified). The Cariow-Hadamard duality between rotation and
algebraic multiplication identifies shared butterfly structure for
potential fused hardware implementations.
