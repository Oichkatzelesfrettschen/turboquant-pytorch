Title: Distribution-Aware KV Cache Compression:
Why Pre-processing Beats Rotation

KV cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference. We show that the
primary quality bottleneck in KV cache quantization is the mismatch
between real cache distributions (per-channel variance ratio up to
423x) and the Gaussian assumption of optimal scalar codebooks.

We present a pipeline that first normalizes the distribution
(Normalize-Shift-Normalize reduces variance ratio to 1.01x), then
applies materialized Walsh-Hadamard rotation via cuBLAS (64x fewer
parameters than dense Haar at equivalent throughput), and finally
bit-packs QJL residual signs (8x sign storage reduction).

Ablation on Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B decomposes each contribution:
NSN pre-processing accounts for +136 basis points attention cosine
similarity and +25 percentage points top-1 match. Rotation method
(Haar, WHT, Cayley-Dickson, Clifford) contributes less than 1bp. The
full pipeline achieves 0.9985 cosine (vs 0.9849 vanilla TurboQuant)
with 69% total KV memory reduction.

We ground the rotation design space in Cayley-Dickson algebra, proving
isometry for quaternion and octonion block rotations via SMT solver
(30 of 36 properties verified), and identify shared Hadamard butterfly
structure between rotation and algebraic multiplication.
