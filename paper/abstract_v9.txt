Title: Architecture-Aware KV Cache Quantization:
Distribution Normalization with Structured Rotation Dispatch

KV cache memory dominates long-context LLM inference and scales
linearly with context length. We present an architecture-aware
quantization pipeline with three contributions:

First, we show that per-channel variance ratios up to 423x in real
KV caches defeat Gaussian-optimal codebooks. Normalize-Shift-Normalize
(NSN) pre-processing reduces this ratio to 1.01x, yielding +136
basis points attention cosine and +25pp top-1 match on Qwen2.5-3B
and Mistral-7B -- the dominant quality contribution in our ablation.

Second, we implement architecture-aware dispatch: materialized
Walsh-Hadamard rotation via cuBLAS with TF32 auto-enable (3x on
Ampere+) at 64x fewer parameters than dense Haar, and bit-packed
QJL signs reducing attention score bandwidth 7.5x (256B to 34B per
score at 3-bit). GPU dispatch selects math mode across five
architecture tiers (Hopper through Turing).

Third, we explore the rotation design space through Cayley-Dickson
algebra, proving isometric guarantees for structured block rotations
(30/36 properties SMT-verified) and identifying shared Hadamard
butterfly structure between rotation and algebraic multiplication.
Surprisingly, rotation choice contributes less than 1bp regardless of
structure -- NSN dominates quality while architecture-aware dispatch
determines throughput.
