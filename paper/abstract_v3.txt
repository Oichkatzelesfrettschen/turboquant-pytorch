Title: Distribution-Aware KV Cache Compression: NSN Pre-processing,
Structured Rotation, and Bit-Packed Residual Correction

Key-value (KV) cache memory is the primary bottleneck for long-context
LLM inference. We show that the dominant quality loss in KV cache
quantization stems from the mismatch between real KV cache distributions
(severe per-channel outliers) and the Gaussian assumption of optimal
scalar codebooks -- not from the rotation or codebook design.

We introduce a three-stage pipeline: (1) Normalize-Shift-Normalize
(NSN) pre-processing transforms KV distributions to KL divergence 0.02
from the codebook design distribution; (2) materialized Walsh-Hadamard
rotation via cuBLAS achieves dense-rotation throughput (561M vectors/s
with TF32) at 64x fewer parameters; (3) bit-packed QJL sign storage
reduces residual correction memory 8x.

On Qwen2.5-3B and Mistral-7B, NSN contributes +136 basis points cosine
similarity and +25 percentage points top-1 attention match -- the
dominant effect. The full pipeline achieves 0.9985 attention cosine
(vs 0.9849 vanilla TurboQuant) with 69% memory reduction.

We analyze the rotation design space through Cayley-Dickson algebra,
proving isometric guarantees for structured block rotations and
identifying shared butterfly structure between Walsh-Hadamard rotation
and algebraic multiplication (Cariow factorization), suggesting fused
hardware implementations exploiting this duality.
